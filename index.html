Industry Experience

While working within a department in IT, I was tasked with developing a one-stop shop web application to showcase the services our team provided to others within IT, and to present interactive API documentation for a new RESTful API for our services.

I interviewed members of my team and others in IT who would be using our services to determine pain points in the current processes, and narrow and define the scope of the project.

I followed an iterative design process, developing initial mockups and prototypes, conducting usability tests, updating my designs, and eventually creating a final look and feel that was both intuitive and fit with our company's UX style.

To illustrate my design process, here are some of the steps I followed when developing the site's interactive API documentation.
Using Adobe Experience Design, I first created three low-fidelity prototypes to explore design possibilities.
I then led coworkers through key tasks in usability tests to determine what was intuitive and what was not.
I also discussed my designs remotely through video conferencing and screensharing, driving meetings about my designs.
My new design included the dropdown aspect most effective for developers and emphasized features too subtle in earlier designs.
Within the interactive view of the API documentation where developers can build various calls, I persisted this dropdown design.
After futher iteration and discussion, I grouped like actions together in the API builder and to facilitate a smoother workflow.

Human-Computer Interaction Coursework

In CSE 440, I worked with three of my peers to design an application that encouraged and facilitated community engagement. We narrowed our focus to supporting community gardens and augmenting their volunteering potential to engage more low-income citizens in the community.

Our work thoroughly introduced best practices for UX design and developement. We conducted user research, formulated early sketches of designs, performed task analysis, developed storyboards, and then created our first low-fidelity prototypes.

These we used in usability tests with members of the community (gardeners, community garden management), and further refined our design. Finally we developed a high-fidelity prototype, and presented our designs to fellow classmates and visiting professionals.

To view our group portfolio and more detail on our project, please visit our website.

A central aspect of our work was conceptualization of our project, its goals and desired impact before we began to design interaction.
After identifying key tasks, we focused on imagining how users of various personas could perform these tasks with our interface.
We developed a thorough paper prototype for both our iPad app and companion iPhone app to use in usability tests.

Architecture Design

My first year of university, I planned to study architecture. One of the prerequisite courses was an architectural drawing course, held in a studio and led as a design workshop. Architectural drawing not only refined my artistic and drafting skills, but also taught me how to function in a studio environment. I learned how to both receive and provide constructive criticism well, and iteratively improve my designs based on feedback. I also gained an understanding of layout, perspective, and composition. This selection of my work illustrates my hand-drawn work, as well as digital overlay and poster design using Adobe Photoshop and Illustrator.

Presentation of a passage in University of Washington’s Gould Hall, drawn from several perspectives and with varying emphasis. Digital overlay on hand-drawn architectural drawings.
Axonometric drawing of a passage in University of Washington’s Gould Hall with digital overlay to emphasize materials and spatial structure.
Architectural style drawings of various arrangements of intricately cut Styrofoam, emphasizing the positive space of the objects.
Drawing of one such Styrofoam arrangement and a solid representation of the negative space it creates. Completed with digital overlay and colors to emphasize structure.
